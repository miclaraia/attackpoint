Sunday Apr 10 #

11 AM add
Edit C

Orienteering 1:16:38 [3] 7.41 km (10:21 / km) +131m 9:30 / km
shoes: VJ Bold 2









Some solid relay training. We were split into teams of 4, and each round the team that got all their people back first got the most points. Then in the fourth round it was a chase start according to the points accrued, with the first team finishing winning.

My GPS failed for the first two loops for some reason so I don't have routes.

I had a solid start to the first loop. I nailed the first two controls, a little wobbly on the third one. The fourth was fine, but then on the fifth I should have stayed on the trail and attacked straight down the riverbed rather than trying to compass my way in from afar. There were way too many riverbeds to make sense of it all crossing the terrain, so the time I could have saved going straight was insignificant to the time I lost having no clue what was where.

Second loop was really good. I was with Sebby almost the entire time, but we alternated who led controls here and there. To 4 I definitely took a slower route, but nailed the control which put me ahead. I went on to nail 5 with three or four people chasing me, but I lost that lead when I didn't see the trail that was RIGHT THERE and bailed to the main trail to the west instead.

Third loop was iffy again. I nailed the first control ahead of Joey, but then I crossed the trail without seeing it and proceeded to cross the second trail in that Y. Lost a couple minutes turned around there before bailing to the trail and relocating. Took it slow and safe the next few controls, definitely too slow and safe but that's what happens after a messup.

I lost a bunch of time on the first control of the last loop. I saw people start up the trail the last few rounds on this loop, so that's what I did even though running west on the trail initially was a much better route. I hit the little trail and had no idea where I was in relation to the rivers. Eventually I had the sense to go to the junction and attack from south west, but that took way too long. The green wasn't actually green there, so leaving north east to the trail adjacent to 2 would have been more sensible. I also lost time to 3 because I tried going through the woods instead of attacking close from the trail, and lost count of the river crossings.

During lunch we got the map and were supposed to draw our routes for each leg. Then we talked it over in small groups to see the different options and discuss the optimal route. In the forest we were split into groups of 2 (3 in my case) and we tested the different route options. Was supposed to be a low intensity training, but that means different things to different people so I think some of our route tests weren't quite accurate.

GPS link, I started in the last group with Florian Attinger and Andrin Gründler.
